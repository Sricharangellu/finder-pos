# Ascend - Engineering Audit 2026-07-04L
> Session focus: Sri-directed retail-first platform scope rewrite
> Claimed by Codex session J
> Scope: WORK documentation only; no app code, backend modules, frontend pages, or e2e specs

---

## Headline

Future development is now explicitly retail-first.

Ascend remains one modular business operating platform for many business types, but the
project must finish one complete business type end-to-end before deepening others.
The first complete business type is **Retail**.

## What changed

- `WORK/RULES.md` now tells every AI agent:
  - Ascend is one platform with business packs, not separate apps.
  - Retail is the current priority business type.
  - No wholesale, restaurant, mobile, grocery, hospitality, golf, healthcare, education,
    entertainment, manufacturing, rental, or other vertical deepening until retail is
    Built and verified end-to-end.
  - Signup, login, setup, settings, and demo/business-mode switching must be driven by a
    capabilities/business-pack source of truth.
- `WORK/FORWARD_PLAN.md` now makes:
  - Phase 2 = Retail release pack.
  - Phase 3 = Business-pack control plane.
  - Phase 4 = Production hardening.
  - Phase 5 = non-retail business expansion.
- `WORK/FORWARD_PLAN.md` now specifies the setup/auth/settings/demo UX:
  - signup creates tenant + owner user;
  - setup selects business type and starts from a curated pack;
  - settings shows current business type, active modules, disabled-module reasons,
    required fields, active workflows, and role/permission changes;
  - demo account can switch business types to preview UI/UX, but only retail may be
    marked Built and verified until retail gates pass.
- `WORK/WORK_STATE.md` now records the retail-first plan as the active development
  policy.

## Required next build direction

The next real implementation should be the business-pack control surface needed by
retail setup and demo switching, not another vertical page:

1. `GET /api/v1/capabilities` for the current tenant/user.
2. Read-only business-type impact preview.
3. Retail setup flow proof: signup/login -> select retail -> configure outlet/register.
4. Retail product/inventory proof: create product -> receive stock.
5. Retail POS proof: open register -> sell -> pay -> receipt -> inventory movement.
6. Retail closeout proof: close register -> end-of-day report -> audit log.
7. Retail refund/return proof.

## Verification

## Verdict

This is the correct scope control. The product vision stays broad, but the build sequence
is now narrow: finish retail first, then use the same proven core and capabilities model
to expand other packs.
